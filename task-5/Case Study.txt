Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. It commonly occurs through emails, messages, or fake websites.

A typical phishing attack begins when a student receives an email or SMS that appears to be from a legitimate organization, such as a bank, university, or popular service like Amazon or Paytm. The message often contains urgent language, such as “Your account will be blocked” or “Verify your details immediately.” It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website that looks identical to the original.

Once the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker collects this information. In some cases, the victim may also be asked to share an OTP, which allows the attacker to complete unauthorized transactions.

Students and young users are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms but may not always verify the authenticity of messages. The consequences of phishing can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with online messages and always verifying the source before sharing any sensitive information.
